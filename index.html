Q1a
Describe the linear and non-linear elements with examples.
Sure Shot 2 marks · CO1 K2
2M

✅ Model Answer

Linear Elements: Elements that obey Ohm's law (V-I relationship is a straight line through origin). Their parameters (R, L, C) remain constant regardless of voltage/current. Examples: Resistor, Inductor, Capacitor.

Non-Linear Elements: V-I characteristic is NOT a straight line. Parameters change with applied voltage/current. Examples: Diode, Transistor, Zener diode, Iron-core inductor.

For linear: V = IR (R = constant) → straight line graph
For non-linear: V ≠ kI (k changes) → curved graph

Derive that average power consumed by a pure inductor is zero.
HOT 2 marks · CO2
2M

✅ Model Answer

For a pure inductor: voltage v = Vm sin(ωt), current lags by 90°

i = Im sin(ωt − 90°) = −Im cos(ωt)

p = −(VmIm/2) × sin(2ωt)

Average power over one complete cycle:

Pavg = (1/T) ∫₀ᵀ p dt = 0
∴ Average power consumed by pure inductor = 0 W ✓

The energy stored in L during +ve half cycle is returned during −ve half cycle → net power = 0

State Parallelogram law and Polygon law of forces.
Sure Shot 2 marks · CO4
2M

✅ Model Answer

Parallelogram Law: If two forces acting at a point are represented in magnitude and direction by two adjacent sides of a parallelogram, their resultant is represented in magnitude and direction by the diagonal of the parallelogram drawn from the same point.

R = √(P² + Q² + 2PQ cosθ)  |  tan α = Q sinθ/(P + Q cosθ)

Polygon Law: If a number of coplanar concurrent forces are represented in magnitude and direction by the sides of a polygon taken in order, their resultant is represented in magnitude and direction by the closing side of the polygon taken in the opposite order.

✅ Model Answer

Gear: A gear is a rotating machine part having cut teeth (cogs) which mesh with another toothed part to transmit torque and speed. Gears change rotational speed, torque, and direction of motion.

Gear Ratio: The ratio of the number of teeth on the driven gear to the number of teeth on the driving gear.

Q6a
Explain resonance in series R-L-C circuit. Derive resonant frequency and quality factor.
🔥 HOTSure Shot 7 marks · CO2 K2
7M

✅ Series Resonance — Full Answer

Series RLC Circuit:

Impedance Z = R + j(X_L − X_C) = R + j(ωL − 1/ωC)

Condition for Resonance: Net reactance = 0

X_L = X_C → ωL = 1/ωC → ω² = 1/LC

Resonant Frequency:

ω₀ = 1/√(LC) rad/s
f₀ = 1/(2π√(LC)) Hz ← LEARN THIS BY HEART

At Resonance:

  • Z = R (minimum, purely resistive)
  • Current I = V/R (maximum)
  • V_L = V_C (but opposite phase, cancel out)
  • Power factor = 1 (unity)

Quality Factor Q:

Q = ω₀L/R = 1/(ω₀CR) = (1/R)√(L/C)

Q represents the ratio of energy stored to energy dissipated per cycle. Higher Q → sharper resonance peak, more selective circuit.

Bandwidth: BW = f₀/Q = R/(2πL)
Half-power frequencies: f₁ = f₀ − BW/2, f₂ = f₀ + BW/2

✅ Parallel Resonance

In parallel RLC: Admittance Y = 1/R + j(1/X_C − 1/X_L) = 1/R + j(ωC − 1/ωL)

Derive EMF equation of generator. A 4-pole generator with 600 armature conductors has useful flux 0.02 Wb/pole, wave wound, runs at 1000 rpm. Find EMF.
🔥 MOST HOTCalculation 7 marks · CO1 K2

✅ EMF Equation of DC Generator

Derivation:

  • Let: P = number of poles, φ = flux per pole (Wb), Z = total armature conductors, N = speed (rpm), A = number of parallel paths
  • Flux cut by one conductor per revolution = P × φ
  • Time for one revolution = 60/N seconds
EMF per conductor = dφ/dt = Pφ/(60/N) = PφN/60

Total conductors in series per path = Z/A

∴ Generated EMF E = (PφN/60) × (Z/A)
E = φZNP/(60A) volts
For Wave winding: A = 2
For Lap winding: A = P

Numerical:

Given: P=4, Z=600, φ=0.02 Wb, N=1000 rpm, Wave wound → A=2

E = (0.02 × 600 × 1000 × 4)/(60 × 2)
E = (0.02 × 600 × 1000 × 4)/120
E = 48000/120 = 400
E = 400 V ✓

Q9a
Describe working principle and torque-slip characteristics of 3-φ induction motor.
🔥 MOST HOTSure Shot 7 marks · CO5 K2
7M

✅ 3-Phase Induction Motor — Must Know!

Working Principle:

  1. 3-phase AC supply is given to 3-phase stator winding
  2. This creates a Rotating Magnetic Field (RMF) in the stator, rotating at synchronous speed Ns = 120f/P
  3. This rotating flux cuts the rotor conductors → EMF is induced by Faraday's law
  4. Since rotor is short-circuited, current flows in rotor → rotor has its own magnetic field
  5. Interaction of stator and rotor fields → torque is produced (Lenz's law: rotor tries to follow stator field)
  6. Rotor rotates at speed Nr < Ns, the difference is called Slip
Slip: s = (Ns − Nr)/Ns × 100%

Torque-Slip Characteristics:

Key Points of T-s Curve:

  • At s=0 (synchronous speed): T=0 (no relative motion, no induced EMF)
  • At s=s_m (slip at max torque): T = T_max (breakdown torque) — motor can handle up to this
  • At s=1 (standstill): Starting torque T_st (usually < T_max)
  • Stable operating region: s = 0 to s_m (normal running region)
  • Unstable region: s = s_m to s = 1
Slip at max torque: s_m = R₂/X₂
Maximum torque: T_max = V₁²/(2ω_s X₂)

IQ1
Explain KCL and KVL with circuit diagrams.
Sure ShotFoundation

✅ Kirchhoff's Laws

KCL (Kirchhoff's Current Law): At any node in a circuit, the algebraic sum of currents meeting at that node is zero.

ΣI = 0 at a node → Currents entering = Currents leaving

Based on conservation of charge.

KVL (Kirchhoff's Voltage Law): In any closed loop of a circuit, the algebraic sum of all the EMFs and voltage drops is zero.

ΣEMF = ΣIR in any closed loop

Based on conservation of energy.

Sign Convention for KVL: If traversal direction is same as current → voltage drop is +ve. If opposite → −ve. EMF: traverse from − to + → +ve.
IQ2
Explain working principle of transformer and derive EMF equation.
🔥 HOT

✅ Transformer — EMF Equation

Principle: Based on Faraday's law of electromagnetic induction and mutual induction. When AC is applied to primary, it creates alternating flux in core. This changing flux links with secondary winding, inducing EMF.

EMF Equation Derivation:

Flux: φ = φ_m sin(ωt)

e₁ = −N₁ dφ/dt = −N₁ φ_m ω cos(ωt) = N₁ φ_m ω sin(ωt−90°)

Peak value: E₁_peak = N₁ ω φ_m = N₁ × 2πf × φ_m

RMS value: E₁ = E₁_peak/√2 = (2πf N₁ φ_m)/√2 = 4.44 f N₁ φ_m
E₁ = 4.44 f N₁ φ_m volts
E₂ = 4.44 f N₂ φ_m volts
Turns ratio: E₁/E₂ = N₁/N₂
IQ3
Why is commutator needed in DC machine?
Sure Short Q

✅ Role of Commutator

The EMF generated in armature conductors of DC generator is actually alternating (AC) in nature. The commutator acts as a mechanical rectifier that converts this AC into unidirectional (DC) output at the brushes.

In DC motor, commutator ensures that the torque produced is always in the same direction (unidirectional), even though supply current is DC — it reverses current in conductors as they pass from one pole to another.

Generator: AC → DC converter (rectifier function)
Motor: Ensures unidirectional torque
IQ4
What happens if transformer primary is connected to DC supply?
Important

✅ DC Supply to Transformer Primary

Transformer works on the principle of changing (alternating) flux. When DC is applied:

  1. DC produces a constant (steady) flux in the core
  2. Since dφ/dt = 0, no EMF is induced in secondary → no output
  3. The primary winding has only its DC resistance (no reactance since f=0 → X_L = 2πfL = 0)
  4. Very high current flows through primary (I = V/R only)
  5. This causes overheating and burning of primary winding
Conclusion: Transformer will be damaged/burnt if DC is applied to primary ⚠️

MQ1
Define Force, Moment, Couple. Four characteristics of force.
Sure Shot

✅ Basics of Mechanics

Force: An external agent which changes or tends to change the state of rest or motion of a body. It is a vector quantity.

Moment: Turning effect of a force about a point. M = Force × Perpendicular distance (N·m)

Couple: Two equal, parallel, opposite forces acting at different points. Moment of couple = F × d (distance between forces). Net force = 0 but moment ≠ 0.

Four Characteristics of Force:

  1. Magnitude — how large the force is (N, kN)
  2. Direction — line of action and sense (angle)
  3. Point of Application — where force acts on body
  4. Nature/Type — push or pull (compressive/tensile)
MQ2
State and Prove Varignon's Theorem.
HOT

✅ Varignon's Theorem

Statement: The moment of a resultant force about any point is equal to the algebraic sum of the moments of its component forces about the same point.

Proof:

Let force F at point A have components F_x and F_y. Let d = perpendicular distance from point O to line of action of F.

Moment of F about O = F × d = F(y cosθ − x sinθ)
Sum of moments of components:
M_x = F_x × y = F cosθ × y
M_y = −F_y × x = −F sinθ × x
Sum = F cosθ × y − F sinθ × x = F(y cosθ − x sinθ)
∴ Moment of F = Sum of moments of F_x and F_y ✓ (Proved)

Application: Used to find resultant force position and moment of concurrent/parallel force systems easily.

MQ4
What are actuators? Explain Gears, Ratchet mechanism, Cam & follower, Kinematic chain.
🔥 HOTUnit 5

✅ Actuators & Mechanisms

Actuators: Devices that convert control signals (electrical/pneumatic/hydraulic) into mechanical motion/action. They are the "muscles" of mechatronic systems.

(i) Gears: Toothed wheels that mesh together to transmit power and change speed/torque/direction. Types: spur, helical, bevel, worm gears. Gear ratio = N_driven/N_driving = T_driving/T_driven.

(ii) Ratchet Mechanism: Allows motion in one direction only, prevents reverse motion. Consists of ratchet wheel (toothed) + pawl (pivoted arm). Used in jacks, wrenches, clocks.

(iii) Cam and Follower: Cam is a non-circular rotating member that gives a predetermined motion to the follower. Converts rotary motion → reciprocating/oscillating motion. Used in IC engine valve timing, textile machines.

(iv) Kinematic Chain: A combination of kinematic pairs (joints) connected in series, where relative motion of the links is possible and predictable. When one link is fixed → mechanism. When closed → four-bar linkage, slider-crank, etc.

MQ5
Explain pressure control valve and 3/2 Pneumatically Actuated Directional Control Valve.
HOTUnit 5

✅ Hydraulic/Pneumatic Valves

Pressure Control Valve: Controls the pressure in a hydraulic/pneumatic circuit. Types:

  • Pressure Relief Valve: Limits maximum system pressure by diverting excess fluid to tank when pressure exceeds set value. Has a spring-loaded ball/poppet. Safety device.
  • Pressure Reducing Valve: Limits pressure in secondary circuit to a set value lower than main circuit.

3/2 DCV (3-port, 2-position, Pneumatically Actuated):

  • 3 ports: P (Pressure/Supply), A (Actuator/Output), R or T (Return/Exhaust)
  • 2 positions: Normal position and actuated position
  • Pneumatically Actuated: Shifted by air pressure signal
  • Position 1 (Normal): A connected to R (exhaust), P blocked
  • Position 2 (Actuated): P connected to A, R blocked

Used to control single-acting cylinders, ON/OFF control of actuators.
